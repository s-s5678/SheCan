Risk Factors

A breast cancer risk factor is anything that makes it more likely you'll get breast cancer. But having one or even several breast cancer risk factors doesn't necessarily mean you'll develop breast cancer. Many women who develop breast cancer have no known risk factors other than simply being women.

A personal history of breast conditions

Inherited genes that increase cancer risk

Radiation exposure

Obesity

Postmenopausal hormone therapy

Drinking alcohol

MYTHS VS. FACTS

  • If I don’t have a family history of breast cancer, I won’t get it.

    • Only about 5–10% of breast cancers are believed to be hereditary, meaning they’re caused by abnormal changes (or mutations) in certain genes passed from parent to child.The vast majority of people who get breast cancer have no family history, suggesting that other factors must be at work, such as environment and lifestyle.

  • If you maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly, eat healthy, and limit alcohol, you don’t have to worry about breast cancer.

    • There is evidence that all of these behaviors can help lower your risk. However, they can’t guarantee you’ll never get the disease. There are so many examples of people who do everything right and still get breast cancer.

  • Wearing a bra can cause breast cancer.

    • The theory was that wearing a bra — especially an underwire style — could restrict the flow of lymph fluid out of the breast, causing toxic substances to build up in the tissue.However, there is no evidence to support this claim.

  • Using underarm antiperspirant can cause breast cancer.

    • There is no evidence of a link between antiperspirant use and breast cancer. Still, some studies have found that women who use aluminum products under their arms are more likely to have higher concentrations of aluminum in breast tissue.

  • Consuming too much sugar causes breast cancer.

    • While it’s true that cancer cells consume sugar more quickly than normal cells, there isn’t any evidence that excessive sugar consumption causes cancer.

  • Breast cancer always causes a lump you can feel.

    • Although most lumps are benign (not cancer), there is always the possibility of breast cancer.
